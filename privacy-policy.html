Privacy Policy

Website: beijinghealthcarenavigation.com
Brand: YuanU Medical Concierge
Operating Entity: Beijing YuanU International Travel Service Co., Ltd. (Travel Agency License No. L-BJ-CJ00262)

We respect your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect when you interact with beijinghealthcarenavigation.com or use our care coordination services, how we use it, with whom we share it, and the rights you have over it. Because we serve inbound clients traveling to China for medical care — many of whom are based in the European Union, the United Kingdom, or the United States (including California) — this policy is written to align with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and the California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA), in addition to applicable laws of the People's Republic of China.

1. Who We Are

YuanU Medical Concierge ("YuanU," "we," "us," "our") is the medical care coordination brand operated by Beijing YuanU International Travel Service Co., Ltd. We coordinate hospital appointments, on-site translation, itinerary planning, and invoice-related assistance for international clients receiving care in Beijing. We are not a hospital, clinic, or medical provider.

For any privacy-related question, please contact us by email (see Section 13). Our website does not publish a telephone number; all privacy inquiries are handled by email.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the information we need to respond to your inquiry and to coordinate your care visit.

2.1 Information You Submit Through the Inquiry Form

When you complete the inquiry form on beijinghealthcarenavigation.com, we collect:
- Your name
- Your email address
- Your country of residence
- A free-text description of the care you are seeking, which you may choose to include health-related details in

You control how much health-related detail you enter. We ask you to share only what is necessary to help us understand your request.

2.2 Information You Provide During Service

Once we begin coordinating your visit, and only if you voluntarily provide it, we may receive:
- Prior medical records, imaging, or physician notes that you choose to send us to share with the treating hospital
- Passport or identity information required by the hospital for registration
- Travel dates and itinerary details relevant to scheduling
- Billing and invoice contact details

We do not request, and you should not send us, more health information than is needed for appointment coordination.

2.3 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit the website, we may automatically receive limited technical data such as IP address, browser type, device type, referring page, and pages viewed. See Section 8 (Cookies) for details.

2.4 Sensitive / Special Category Data

Health-related information is treated as a special category of personal data under GDPR and as sensitive personal information under CPRA. We process it only with your explicit consent and only for the coordination purposes described in Section 3.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information strictly to:
- Respond to your inquiry
- Coordinate appointments with hospitals, including Beijing Friendship Hospital – International Medical Department (BFH-IMD), where we facilitate visits
- Arrange on-site interpretation and companion services
- Assist with travel and itinerary planning around your medical appointments
- Support invoice and receipt handling
- Communicate with you about the status of your case
- Meet legal, tax, and record-keeping obligations that apply to us

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your information for behavioral advertising. We do not share your information with data brokers or marketing networks.

Legal Bases (GDPR)

Depending on the interaction, we rely on the following legal bases:
- Consent — for processing health-related information and for non-essential cookies
- Performance of a contract — to deliver the coordination services you have requested
- Legitimate interests — to operate and secure the website, and to respond to inquiries
- Legal obligation — for tax, accounting, and regulatory record-keeping

4. Sharing With Third Parties

We share personal information only where necessary and only with the following categories of recipients:

4.1 Partner Hospitals

We share only the specific information needed to register your appointment with the hospital you have chosen — for example, BFH-IMD. Sharing of health information with a hospital takes place only after your written consent (typically by email confirmation).

4.2 Service Providers

We use a small number of standard business tools (email hosting, cloud file storage, website analytics) whose providers process limited data on our behalf under confidentiality obligations. We do not authorize them to use your data for their own purposes.

4.3 Legal and Regulatory

We may disclose information where required by applicable law, court order, or lawful regulatory request.

4.4 No Sale, No Advertising Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We do not share personal information with advertising networks. This includes "sale" and "sharing" as defined under the CCPA/CPRA.

5. Data Storage and Retention

5.1 Storage Location

Personal information collected through this website is stored on servers located in the People's Republic of China.

5.2 Retention Period

We retain personal information for up to twelve (12) months after the conclusion of your case, unless a longer period is required by law (for example, tax records). After this period, information is securely deleted or irreversibly anonymized.

You may request earlier deletion at any time — see Section 6.

6. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right of access — to obtain a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — to request deletion of your information
  • Right to data portability — to receive your information in a commonly used, machine-readable format
  • Right to restrict processing — to limit how we use your information in certain circumstances
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, at any time, without affecting prior processing
  • Right to lodge a complaint — with your local data protection authority (for EU residents) or the California Privacy Protection Agency (for California residents)

For California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

You additionally have the right to know the categories of personal information collected, the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights, and the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information. As stated above, we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

How to Exercise Your Rights

Send your request to contact@yuanu.com. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (generally within 30 days under GDPR, 45 days under CCPA). We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests.

7. International Data Transfers

Because our operations are based in China and you may be located in the EU, the UK, or the US, your personal information will be transferred to and processed in China. We rely on the following mechanisms and safeguards:

  • Your explicit consent to the transfer, after you have been informed of this policy
  • Where applicable, Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent instruments with third-party service providers
  • Technical and organizational measures — access controls, encryption in transit, need-to-know handling — designed to protect your data during and after transfer

You have the right to request further information about the safeguards in place by contacting us at contact@yuanu.com.

8. Cookies

Our website uses only strictly necessary cookies required for the website to function (for example, remembering your cookie preferences). These do not require consent.

We do not use analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or cross-site tracking cookies. Google Analytics and similar third-party analytics services are not deployed on this website.

You can clear cookies at any time through your browser settings.

9. Minors

Our services are intended for adults. If the person seeking care is under 18 years of age, the inquiry and any related personal information must be submitted by a parent or legal guardian, who is responsible for providing consent on the minor's behalf. If we learn that we have collected information directly from a minor without appropriate guardian consent, we will delete it.

10. Data Security

We apply reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information, including:
- Restricted access on a need-to-know basis
- Encrypted transmission of files where feasible
- Secure email handling for sensitive attachments
- Periodic review of retention and deletion

No system is perfectly secure. We ask you to send only the information that is necessary and to use secure channels when sharing health records.

11. Data Breach Notification

If we become aware of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours where required, and we will notify affected individuals without undue delay in accordance with applicable law.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our services, technology, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the bottom of this page and, where appropriate, provide additional notice by email or on the website.
